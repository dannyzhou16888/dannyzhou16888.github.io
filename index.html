rocket history Rocket Principles

A rocket in its simplest form is a chamber enclosing a gas under pressure.

A small opening at one end of the chamber allows the gas to escape,

and in doing so provides a thrust that propels the rocket in the opposite direction.

A good example of this is a balloon. Air inside a balloon is compressed by the balloon's rubber walls.

The air pushes back so that the inward and outward pressing forces are balanced.

When the nozzle is released, air escapes through it and the balloon is propelled in the opposite direction.
